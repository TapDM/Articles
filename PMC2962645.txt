     Abstract
     Background: Prion protein (PRNP/Prp) plays crucial role transmissible spongiform encephalopathies (TSEs) like
     Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease (CJD), scrapie mad cow disease. Notwithstanding importance human animal
     disease, fundamental aspects PRNP/Prp function transmission remains unaccounted 

     Methodology/Principal Findings: zebrafish (Danio rerio) genome contains Prp encoding genes assigned prp1,
     prp2 prp3. Currently, second paralogue believed similar mammalian PRNP gene structure
     function. Functional studies PRNP gene ortholog addressed prp2 morpholino (MO) knockdown
     experiments. Investigation Prp2 depleted embryos revealed high mortality apoptosis 24 hours post fertilization
     (hpf) impaired brain neuronal development. order elucidate underlying mechanisms, genome-
     wide transcriptome analysis carried viable 24 hpf morphants. resulting changes gene expression profiles
     revealed 249 differently expressed genes linked biological processes like cell death, neurogenesis embryonic
     development.

     Conclusions/Significance: current study contributes understanding basic Prp functions demonstrates      zebrafish excellent model address role Prp vertebrates. gene knockdown prp2 indicates      essential biological function zebrafish ortholog morphant phenotype suggests neurodegenerative
     action gene expression effects apoptosis related effects gene networks controlling neurogenesis      embryo development.
